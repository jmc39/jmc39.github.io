leaflet.js vs. Tableau mapping tool:
Purpose: *External* facing GID explorer mock-up developed as "automated" alternative to Tableau-based map, which requires push updates. Given the “live connection” limitations of selene Tableau Server (and other Tableau limitations), leaflet.js maps are under development.>

Aside: Using Tableau for the *internal* facing GID map to support CAO litigation research is still in pursuit because of the Tableau lasso feature. Recall that the lasso feature allows CAO staff to filter (via free-form, circle, or box outline) and download this filtered data, which is most relevant to the litigation. CAO litigation research does not require a “live data connection” since the slip-trip-fall incident occurred in the past (i.e. the incident date is the timestamp end point for relevant GID cases to download). In short, CAO staff can be trained to DIY GID case research using Tableau desktop (and with no need for to spin up selene and incur utilization charges). In the end, a DIY approach can hopefully relieve PandA Tech&Innovation peeps from doing the GID case research for the CAO staff.
